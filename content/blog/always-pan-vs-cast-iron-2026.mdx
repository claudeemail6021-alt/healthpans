## The Safety Comparison

Both are genuinely non-toxic — but in different ways.

**Lodge Cast Iron** has no coatings at all. It's iron and vegetable oil. There's nothing to degrade, flake, or leach synthetic chemicals. The only consideration is the minor iron leaching with acidic foods (tomatoes, wine) — a trace nutritional benefit for most people, a consideration for those with hemochromatosis.

**Our Place Always Pan** uses a PFAS-free ceramic coating — verified by independent testing labs SGS and Intertek. The ceramic is sol-gel based (silica-derived), not fluoropolymer-based, and contains no PTFE, PFOA, PFAS, lead, or cadmium. Our Place publishes their testing certificates publicly.

**Safety verdict:** Both are safe. If you want the most absolutely zero-synthetic-anything option, cast iron wins by definition — there's simply nothing to worry about. If you want verified-safe non-stick performance, the Always Pan delivers it.

## The Performance Comparison

**Where the Always Pan wins:**

- Effortless food release from day one, no technique required
- Lighter weight — easier to handle for most people
- The built-in features (spatula rest, steamer basket, modular lid) are genuinely useful
- Looks beautiful on a stovetop — 10+ colorways
- Works on induction

**Where cast iron wins:**

- Can handle any heat — stovetop to 500°F oven to campfire without any concern
- Gets better with every use for decades
- Costs $30 once and lasts a lifetime; the Always Pan's ceramic degrades in 2–3 years
- Produces a better sear on meat — the heavier mass retains heat better when cold food hits the pan
- No fragility — you can't damage cast iron's cooking surface with metal utensils, high heat, or vigorous cleaning

## Who Should Choose Each

**Choose the Always Pan if:**
- You want the easiest possible transition from Teflon — familiar non-stick performance without PFAS
- You live in a small apartment and want one pan that does everything
- You cook a lot of eggs, fish, and delicate foods and want guaranteed food release every time
- Aesthetics matter to you — the Always Pan is genuinely beautiful on the stovetop

**Choose cast iron if:**
- You want the most absolutely non-toxic option with zero coatings or additives
- You sear meat regularly and want professional-quality crust
- You want a pan that improves over time and lasts generations
- Budget is a consideration — $30 vs. $150 is significant, and you won't need to replace it in 2 years

## The Real Answer: You Might Want Both

They solve different problems. Cast iron is a forever searing and baking pan. The Always Pan is a convenient everyday multi-tasker with non-stick performance. Many cooks find that together, they cover every situation — and the combined cost ($180) is still less than a single All-Clad pan.

If you have to choose just one: **cast iron for the long term, Always Pan for the short term.** Cast iron's seasoning takes a few months to develop full non-stick properties. If you need non-stick performance immediately without a learning curve, start with the Always Pan, then add cast iron once you're ready for the technique investment.
