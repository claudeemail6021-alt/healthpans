**The adjustment:** Cast iron isn't as effortlessly non-stick as Teflon out of the box. But with proper preheat (medium-low for 3–5 minutes), adequate oil, and patience during the first 20–30 uses while the seasoning develops, most foods release without sticking. By month three of daily use, scrambled eggs will slide right out.

## What You Don't Need to Replace Immediately

**Stainless steel pots and pans** are already non-toxic if they're 18/10 grade stainless. There's no urgency to replace these — they're one of the safest materials available.

**Cast iron you already own** (including inherited pieces) — if it's bare cast iron with intact seasoning, it's fine. Use it.

**Sheet pans and baking trays** — if they're plain aluminum or stainless, they're low risk. Prioritize replacing non-stick sheet pans if heavily scratched.
